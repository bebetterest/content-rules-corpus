&sect;1344. Bank fraud

Whoever knowingly executes, or attempts to execute, a scheme or artifice-

(1) to defraud a financial institution; or

(2) to obtain any of the moneys, funds, credits, assets, securities, or other property owned by, or under the custody or control of, a financial institution, by means of false or fraudulent pretenses, representations, or promises;

shall be fined not more than $1,000,000 or imprisoned not more than 30 years, or both.

(Added [Pub. L. 98&ndash;473, title II, &sect;1108(a), Oct. 12, 1984, 98 Stat. 2147](https://uscode.house.gov/statviewer.htm?volume=98&page=2147) ; amended [Pub. L. 101&ndash;73, title IX, &sect;961(k), Aug. 9, 1989, 103 Stat. 500](https://uscode.house.gov/statviewer.htm?volume=103&page=500) ; [Pub. L. 101&ndash;647, title XXV, &sect;2504(j), Nov. 29, 1990, 104 Stat. 4861](https://uscode.house.gov/statviewer.htm?volume=104&page=4861) .)

Editorial Notes

Amendments

1990 - Pub. L. 101&ndash;647 substituted "30" for "20" before "years".

1989 - Pub. L. 101&ndash;73 amended section generally, restating former subsec. (a) and striking out former subsec. (b) which defined "federally chartered or insured financial institution". Prior to amendment, subsec. (a) read as follows: "Whoever knowingly executes, or attempts to execute, a scheme or artifice-

"(1) to defraud a federally chartered or insured financial institution; or

"(2) to obtain any of the moneys, funds, credits, assets, securities or other property owned by or under the custody or control of a federally chartered or insured financial institution by means of false or fraudulent pretenses, representations, or promises, shall be fined not more than $10,000, or imprisoned not more than five years, or both."
